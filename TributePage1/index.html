Betty White

About Betty White



Betty White, in full Betty Marion White, (January 17, 1922 - December 31, 2021), American actress best known for her comedic work on numerous television sitcoms, most notably The Mary Tyler Moore Show and The Golden Girls. Betty had three marriages in her life. After divorced her first husband Dick Barker, she married to her third and last husband Allen Ludden in 1963. While they didn’t have children together, White was the stepmother of Ludden’s three children. She ever remarried after Allen Ludden passed away. In the 1940s she acted on various radio shows, and in 1949 she began regularly appearing on television. While developing her own projects in 1950s, she starred in TV series Date with the Angels and became a frequent guest on television game shows, including To Tell the Truth, What’s My Line?, and Password. Besides so many TV shows she had stared, including The Golden Girls and The Mary Tyler Moore Show, she also appeared in several movies including Advise & Consent, the thriller Lake Placid (1999) and the romantic comedies. In addition to her acting, White was noted as an animal rights activist, a supporter of LGBT rights and an advocate against racism.

Achievements & Awards



Besides winning and being nominated for about 70 times, White had won the follwoing major awards:

  • Five Primetime Emmy Awards
  • Two Daytime Emmy Awards
  • Three American Comedy Awards
  • Three times Gold Derby Awards
  • Three times TV Land Awards
  • Three times Screen Actors Guild Awards
